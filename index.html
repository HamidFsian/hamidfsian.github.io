About Me

I design imaging systems that see beyond human perception. My research sits at the intersection of computational optics, signal processing, and deep learning — specifically applied to Spectral Filter Array (SFA) cameras, sensors that capture 9–16 wavelength bands simultaneously.

My PhD produced the first unified framework for joint demosaicking, super-resolution, and material appearance estimation from SFA video sequences — work now deployed in a lunar rover camera pipeline at CNES, in collaboration with CEA and the Mohammed bin Rashid Space Centre.

On the industrial side, I lead a team building AI-powered dental CBCT analysis used in clinics worldwide, deploying 3D segmentation and treatment planning tools from first principles to production.

Spectral Camera Pipeline for RASHID-3 Lunar Rover

As a CNES-funded postdoctoral fellow, in collaboration with CEA and the Mohammed bin Rashid Space Centre (MBRSC), I develop the full spectral image reconstruction pipeline for the SFA camera onboard the RASHID-2 and RASHID-3 lunar rovers. The camera captures multi-band imagery of the lunar surface to analyse soil mineralogy and regolith composition in unprecedented spectral detail.

  • Absolute radiometric & spectral calibration of SFA sensor under vacuum/thermal conditions
  • Deep learning demosaicking adapted to extreme lunar illumination contrasts
  • Robust reconstruction under dust interference and partial sensor occlusion
  • Collaborative pipeline integration with CEA hardware team and MBRSC mission engineers

Unified SFA Image Reconstruction & Material Appearance Estimation

My doctoral thesis produced a unified deep learning framework that jointly solves demosaicking, super-resolution, and SVBRDF estimation from spectral filter array video sequences. The work was conducted at the Université de Bourgogne with Prof. Jon Y. Hardeberg (NTNU) and Prof. Pierre Gouton, and directly informed the CNES mission pipeline.

  • Two-branch architecture with attention-based spectral fusion and pixel-shuffle upsampling
  • Multi-frame alignment & stitching using homography on selected spectral bands
  • Replaced RGB backbones with SwinUNet + spectral-aware attention for SVBRDF estimation
  • Visiting researcher at NTNU Gjøvik — collaboration with color science group
